Global Superstore Orders

Here is my work in visualizing the data of Global Superstore Orders with Tableau.



From this chart, we can see that the store has made far more profit from the states of California and New York in the last year than any other state. Some states on the other end, are in fact losing profit, the highest of them being Texas, losing the store more than $25,000 in the last year.

To view in Tableau Public, click here.



This graph shows us that the store's sales in California have been on a mostly uphill climb, as we can see from the fact that the two lowest sales months over the past two years were January and February of 2013. On the other hand, the graph seems to widely vary in some areas, such as the fact that in September 2013 was a high selling month, then drops to a low selling month in October, and then jumps to November, the highest selling month of the past two years.

To view in Tableau Public, click here.



In the store's highest profiting state, California, the top category being sold is Office Supplies, then followed by technology, and lastly furniture. Within California, this is a reasonable explanation. People are unlikely to buy much furniture due to the fact that the housing market is California is very expensive, and many of the people who live there have residence in apartments, therefore not having room for furniture. Due to the price of technology today people are hesitant to buy but will most-likely buy if they see the value.

Over the next year, we expect the store to continue its uphill climb in sales, peaking at 191 thousand dollars in November.
